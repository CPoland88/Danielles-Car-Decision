5 · How old is too old?

Tesla doesn’t do tidy model years — it changes parts mid-production — but there’s one breakpoint that matters more than any other for a daily driver in a Maryland winter: the heat pump. Earlier cars use a resistive heater that's far less efficient in the cold, cutting winter range. That arrived with the 2021 refresh on the Model 3, and was standard from the start on the Model Y (launched 2020). Here's the practical read.

The takeaway for these two cars: the 2019 Model 3 is the cheaper sticker but the older design — no heat pump means worse winter efficiency on Sergei’s long daily commute. The 2021 Model Y has the heat pump and more space, but sits in the Model Y’s weakest reliability year, so the certified warranty and a clean recall/valve-fix check matter most there. Neither is disqualifying — but if budget stretches, a 2022 of either is the cleaner buy.

7 · The five things to actually check

The spreadsheet is the easy part. A used EV has a few specific landmines a used gas car doesn't. Walk this list before signing anything.

01

Battery health & warranty

The battery is the engine. Most Teslas carry an 8-year / 100K–120K-mile battery warranty — find out how much is left, because a battery is the one repair that can cost more than the car. Ask for a recent range/health readout and check the in-car battery screen.

02

Charging reality at home

The cheap charging in this calculator assumes home charging. If Sergei would rely on public fast-chargers, running cost climbs and the EV advantage shrinks. A Level 2 home charger runs ~$1,500 installed (~$800 net after the Maryland rebate) — a one-time cost that’s already in the charging line above.

03

Finance the asset, not your patience

Match the loan term to how long you'll keep the car. A 72-month loan on a car you sell in year 4 means owing more than it's worth. Your 4.50% credit-union rate is genuinely good — use it, and keep the term as short as the payment allows.

04

Get the insurance quote first

Before you fall in love with a specific car, get a real insurance quote on it. EV premiums can surprise you. Five minutes now prevents a monthly-budget surprise later.

05

Depreciation already happened — that's the opportunity

The brutal first-owner depreciation on EVs is exactly why a 3–4-year-old used Tesla can be a smart buy: someone else ate the drop. Just don't overpay relative to a comparable gas car, and the math tends to favor holding it a while.
